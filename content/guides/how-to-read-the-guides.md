# How to Read the Guides

LAOS guides are not meant to be consumed like a single long manual. They are built as decision paths that help people move from curiosity to action with as little hesitation as possible.

## Start with the question you are trying to answer

Before you begin, decide which of these goals matches your situation:

- You want to discover better open-source projects.
- You want to evaluate whether a repository is worth your time.
- You want to make your first useful contribution.

The fastest useful route is almost always better than reading every page in order.

## Use the site in layers

The guide layer is the thing that gives direction. It tells you where to begin and what to open next.

- Use **Explore** when you need orientation.
- Use **Projects** when you want to compare repositories more seriously.
- Use **Articles** when you need tradeoffs, background, or deeper thinking.
- Come back to **Guides** whenever you need to decide your next step.

## Treat cards as entry points

Cards inside the Getting Started area are not meant to summarize the whole platform. Their job is to move you into the next useful section quickly.

If a card helps you make the next decision, it is doing its job.

## Do not stay lost in deep dives

After reading a project overview or article, return to the guide route and ask:

1. Do I understand enough to continue?
2. Do I need to compare one more option?
3. Am I ready to contribute or take notes?

That pattern keeps the platform useful instead of turning it into endless browsing.

## Recommended first path

If you are new to LAOS, use this order:

1. Read the Explore section to understand what the platform is trying to surface.
2. Open the project criteria to learn how LAOS judges quality.
3. Return to Guides and choose the path that matches your next action.

That gives you context first, then standards, then momentum.
