# Explain how teams judge repository health

This topic should become an article about practical trust signals, not abstract best practices.

## Core question

How does a team decide whether a repository is alive, maintainable, and worth their time?

## The article should discuss signals such as

- release rhythm
- issue response patterns
- documentation quality
- contribution clarity
- how often the same confusion seems to repeat

## The article should help the reader do something

A good version of this piece gives the reader a checklist or evaluation frame they can reuse on their own.

## What to avoid

- treating stars or popularity as enough evidence
- writing a generic OSS best-practices article
- talking about health without connecting it to real adoption risk
